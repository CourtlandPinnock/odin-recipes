French Toast

What is French Toast?

French toast is a classic dish made from thick sliced bread soaked in mixture of beaten eggs and milk, then pan fried. Served with with a wide range of different toppings and additions, from berries to bacon.

French toast which resembles the dish we know today, has been around since as early as the roman empire. In french, the dish is called pain perdu, or "lost bread" harking back to a time where food scarcity was a reality for many. Rather than wasting the bread that had gone stale, it could be used to make pain perdu.

Instructions

  1. Beat together the eggs, milk, cream, vanilla and cinnamon.
  2. In a shallow dish let the brioche slices soak in the egg mixture for 2-3 minutes, then carefully turn and further soak for 2 more minutes.
  3. Heat 1 tbsp of the oil and butter in a frying pan over a medium heat until foaming. Lift two slices of the brioche and add to the frying pan, make sure to take care when doing this.
  4. Fry until golden, roughly 3 minutes on each side. Place in a warm oven while you repeat for the rest.
  5. Serve hot and with your favorite toppings and syrup.
